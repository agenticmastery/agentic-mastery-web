Lesson: Advanced Callbacks in CXAS

Generative AI models are powerful but inherently probabilistic. They are excellent at understanding context and generating natural language, but they can occasionally fail to follow strict rules, especially when it comes to negative constraints (e.g., "never do X") or mandatory actions (e.g., "always say Y first").

To solve this, CX Agent Studio provides **Callbacks**. Callbacks are hooks that allow you to execute custom code or logic at specific points in the agent's execution lifecycle, outside the core LLM generation loop. This gives you deterministic control over the conversation flow.

Types of Callbacks

  • before_model_callback: This hook fires *before* the model processes the user input and conversation history. It is ideal for:
    • Inspecting and sanitizing user input.
    • Modifying the agent's prompt dynamically based on session variables.
    • Intercepting execution entirely to force a specific tool call or handoff (Handoff Intercept Pattern).
  • after_model_callback: This hook fires *after* the model has generated a response but *before* it is sent back to the user. It is ideal for:
    • Inspecting the model's output for compliance or safety violations.
    • Prepending or appending mandatory text (like disclaimers).
    • Updating session variables based on the model's response.

The 'Why' from the LLM Perspective

Why are callbacks necessary? From the LLM's perspective, every turn is a new generation task based on the provided prompt and history. While we can give it strong instructions like "Always include a disclaimer in your first response," the model's attention mechanism might prioritize other parts of the prompt or the user's immediate query. Callbacks act as a hard, deterministic wrapper around the LLM. They don't rely on the model's "understanding" or "attention"; they operate on the data flowing into or out of the model, ensuring that critical business rules are enforced with 100% reliability.

Common Patterns

1. **Always Read Disclaimer as First Message**: An after_model_callback checks if this is the first turn of the conversation. If it is, and the model did not include the mandatory disclaimer, the callback inserts it automatically.

2. **Dynamic Prompts**: A before_model_callback reads session variables (e.g., the user's loyalty tier) and injects specific instructions into the prompt before it is sent to the LLM.

Pro-Tip: Best Practice

Use callbacks for *deterministic* rules and LLMs for *probabilistic* understanding. Don't try to make the LLM a rigid rule-follower via prompt engineering alone if a callback can do it with 100% reliability. Keep your callbacks focused and lightweight to avoid introducing latency.

Watch-Out: Pitfall

Be careful not to create infinite loops or break the conversation flow in callbacks. For example, if an after_model_callback always prepends a message and triggers another model turn, it might loop indefinitely. Always ensure clear exit conditions.
