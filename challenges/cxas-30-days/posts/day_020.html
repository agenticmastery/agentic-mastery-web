Detailed Lesson: Telephony Deployment in CXAS

CX Agent Studio supports seamless integration with major telephony and Contact Center as a Service (CCaaS) providers. This allows you to assign a phone number to your agent, enabling customers to call and interact with it using natural speech.

Supported integrations include:

  • Google Telephony Platform
  • Twilio
  • AudioCodes
  • Five9
  • Google Cloud CCaaS

The deployment process typically involves configuring a phone number in the provider's platform and mapping it to your CXAS agent via webhooks or direct integration settings. Once connected, incoming calls are routed to the agent, which processes the audio stream and responds in real-time.

The 'Why' from the LLM Perspective

Voice interaction imposes strict latency requirements. In text chat, a delay of a few seconds is tolerable. In a phone call, a silence of more than a second feels awkward and unnatural. Traditional voice systems used a "3-way cascade": Speech-to-Text (STT) -> LLM -> Text-to-Speech (TTS). This introduced significant latency at each step.

To solve this, CXAS modernizes the pipeline. It uses a streamlined **2-way cascade architecture** where streaming audio is fed directly into the LLM (Audio-to-Text), reducing the steps and latency. Furthermore, developed in collaboration with Google DeepMind, CXAS support **Native Audio (Audio-to-Audio)** models. These models process audio directly from input to output, capturing human nuances like tone, pitch, and emotion without the lag of text conversion. This bi-directional streaming architecture is crucial for maintaining the flow of natural conversation on the phone.

Pro-Tip: Voice Prompt Design

Keep voice prompts short and conversational. Avoid reading out long lists of options or complex instructions. Users cannot "scan" audio like they scan text. If you must present options, limit them to three or four at most, and use clear, simple language.

Watch-Out: The Latency Trap

High latency is the enemy of good voice experiences. If your agent takes too long to process a tool call or generate a response, the user might think the call dropped or start speaking again, causing "barge-in" issues. Always optimize your tools and use streaming responses where possible.
