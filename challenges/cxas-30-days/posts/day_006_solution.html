Reference Reflection: Adapting to a High-Tech Support Domain

Here are some reference points for how you might adapt the EcoShop FAQ agent architecture for a high-tech gadget company support agent:

1. Persona Adaptation

  • EcoShop Persona: Friendly, professional, and environmentally conscious. Tone is warm and helpful.
  • High-Tech Support Persona: Precise, authoritative, and tech-savvy. Tone is efficient and reassuring. The focus shifts from "eco-conscious" to "expert problem solver."

2. New Constraints

In a high-tech domain, constraints become even more critical to ensure safety and accuracy:

  • "Do not provide instructions for opening the device casing, as this voids the warranty."
  • "Always verify the specific model number before providing troubleshooting steps."
  • "If the user reports smoke or sparks, immediately stop troubleshooting and provide the emergency safety protocol."

3. Few-Shot Examples for Troubleshooting

Instead of simple policy lookups, examples should show multi-turn troubleshooting:


<example>
    <user>My screen is black on my Gizmo X1.</user>
    <thought>The user is reporting a common issue. I need to check if they have performed basic troubleshooting first, as per constraints.</thought>
    <assistant>I'm sorry to hear that your Gizmo X1 screen is black. Let's try to resolve this. First, have you tried holding down the power button for 10 seconds to perform a hard reset?</assistant>
    <user>Yes, I tried that and nothing happened.</user>
    <thought>The user tried hard reset. Now I should use the tool to search for advanced troubleshooting steps for 'Gizmo X1 black screen' in the Data Store.</thought>
    <call>{@TOOL: data_store_search}(query="Gizmo X1 black screen troubleshooting")</call>
    ...
</example>

Knowledge Check Answers

  1. Which tag in the PIF structure is used to define the guardrails and rules the agent must follow?
    Answer: B) <constraints>
    Explanation: The <constraints> tag is specifically designed to hold the rules, negative constraints, and guardrails that guide the agent's behavior and prevent it from going off-track.
  2. What is the primary benefit of using a Data Store instead of putting all information in the prompt?
    Answer: B) It prevents hallucinations by grounding the agent in factual data retrieved dynamically.
    Explanation: By offloading factual knowledge to a Data Store, you ensure the agent retrieves current, accurate information rather than relying on the model's parametric memory, which can lead to hallucinations.
  3. When are Few-Shot Examples most useful?
    Answer: B) For guiding complex behavior and handling edge cases that are hard to describe with pure rules.
    Explanation: Few-shot examples give the model concrete patterns to follow, which is much more effective for complex edge cases and tone maintenance than trying to write exhaustive rules.
